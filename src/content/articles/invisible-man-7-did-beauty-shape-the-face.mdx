*What follows is a hypothesis — an attempt to think through an interesting possibility rather than assert something the evidence does not yet support. Treat it as a question worth asking, not a conclusion.*

---

## What we can observe

Look at photographs of people from East Asia and Northern Europe side by side and certain physical differences are consistent enough to notice. East Asian faces tend toward smoother skin, rounder facial structure, larger eyes relative to face size, and less pronounced jaw and brow angularity. European faces tend toward stronger jaw structure, more prominent brow bones, and greater physical distinction between male and female features.

Scientists have a term for the retention of juvenile physical features into adulthood: *neoteny*. A neotenous face is one that retains qualities we associate with youth — softness, roundness, smoothness. East Asian populations tend, on average, toward more neotenous facial features than European populations. This is a straightforward biological observation, not a value judgment.

---

## The climate explanation

The mainstream scientific explanation for this is climatic. Northeast Asian populations lived for thousands of years in cold, harsh environments. Flatter facial profiles reduce exposed surface area in freezing temperatures. The epicanthic fold protects eyes from wind, cold, and glare. These are adaptations shaped by environment over tens of thousands of years. That explanation is solid and broadly accepted.

---

## The speculative part

But here is where the hypothesis begins.

The previous article described how East Asian agricultural cultures selected over generations for cooperative, harmonious behaviour. These same cultures developed aesthetic preferences that emphasised softness, refinement, and approachability. Neotenous features — smooth skin, rounder faces, gentler features — were considered beautiful. In Japan an entire aesthetic vocabulary, *kawaii*, is essentially built around celebrating neotenous qualities.

> Is it possible that these aesthetic preferences, sustained over many generations, acted as a secondary selection pressure — reinforcing and deepening the neotenous features that climate had already produced?

Sexual selection is a real evolutionary mechanism. Culture decides what is beautiful. What is considered beautiful gets selected for. Over enough generations that selection leaves physical traces.

---

## The honest answer

We do not know. The climatic explanation alone accounts for most of what we observe. But the reinforcement loop is at least coherent: environment shapes features, culture finds those features beautiful, preference then deepens them further.

The mirror question is equally interesting. Did sustained Western preference for physically dominant, mature male features similarly reinforce what European environments had already produced — selecting away from neoteny rather than toward it?

Two landscapes. Two survival strategies. Two ideals of beauty. And possibly two different faces as a result.
