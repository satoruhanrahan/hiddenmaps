# Built to Survive, Not to See

Your brain is not trying to show you the truth. It is trying to keep you alive. Those two things are not the same, and the difference explains a lot about why people believe what they believe.

The brain burns about twenty percent of your resting energy. That is a huge cost for one organ, so it is under constant pressure to run efficiently. The way it does that is shortcuts. Instead of carefully reasoning through every situation, it pattern-matches, makes fast assumptions, and fills in gaps with whatever it expects to be there. Most of the time this works well enough. And well enough at low cost beats accurate at high cost, at least from a survival standpoint.

This is why your eyes lie to you constantly. What you see feels like a direct feed from reality but it is actually a model your brain has constructed, smoothed over, and filled in. There is a blind spot in each eye where the optic nerve connects. You never see it because the brain just patches over it with whatever surrounds it. You are not perceiving the world so much as experiencing your brain's best guess about it.

Emotions work the same way. Fear, disgust, the instinct to trust people who look like you — these are not bugs. They are fast heuristics that kept your ancestors alive long enough to have children. They arrive before you have time to think, because in the environments they evolved for, thinking took too long.

The uncomfortable part is that this applies to reasoning too. Research on how people form beliefs suggests that in many cases the conclusion comes first and the reasoning gets assembled afterwards. You feel certain about something, then your brain constructs a logical-sounding explanation for why you are right. It does this so smoothly that you experience it as having reasoned your way there.

None of this makes you stupid or broken. It makes you human. The brain is an extraordinary survival machine. It is just not primarily built to find the truth, and pretending otherwise is probably the most reliable way to fool yourself.
