# The First Kami

The Kojiki opens with a list of names.

Before the islands of Japan exist, before the gods of storm and sun and sea have any stories to tell, before anything has happened at all — five kami appear in the Plain of High Heaven and then vanish. They are called the Kotoamatsukami, the Separate Heavenly Deities, and the text is almost perversely brief about them. They emerge. They hide their forms. They are gone.

The first of the five is Amenominakanushi — the Lord of the August Centre of Heaven.

He does not make anything. He does not speak. He does not meet anyone. He simply appears, first of all things, and then the text moves on. He has no mythology, no descendants in the main narrative, no shrine in the ancient records, no clan that claims him as ancestor. He is the first and the most completely absent figure in the entire tradition.

This combination — absolute precedence, absolute silence — is the most deliberate thing about him.

He is not absent because he was forgotten. He is absent because absence is what he is. The first principle. The unified source before differentiation. The still point at the centre of everything that follows. His name means centre — not a direction, not a quality, not a story, but a position. The position from which all other positions radiate.

The closest parallel in Chinese thought is the Tao — the nameless, formless ground that precedes all things and from which all things emerge. The Tao Te Ching's most famous observation is that the Tao which can be named is not the eternal Tao. Amenominakanushi has a name, but the name points at something that cannot be further described. Not a deity who acts. Not a tradition that can be claimed. A pure cosmological position.

He is the cosmos before it has a character.

Most readers of Japanese mythology skip past him quickly, impatient to reach the dramatic parts. This is a mistake. Amenominakanushi is the most politically important figure in the entire Kojiki — not because of what he does but because of what his position makes possible.
