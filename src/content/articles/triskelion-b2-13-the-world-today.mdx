# The World Today

The three-pole framework is not just a historical tool. It is a diagnostic — a way of identifying which pole is dominant in any given civilization, which poles are being suppressed, and what the consequences of that imbalance are likely to be.

Every nation contains all three poles. What follows describes dominant orientations — which pole's values, institutions, and trust systems have most thoroughly shaped each national culture. The mapping is approximate and the categories are ideal types rather than precise descriptions of complex realities.

## The Sea People nations

The United Kingdom — historically the most complete Sea People civilization. Common law, parliamentary democracy, the financial system, the stock market — all Sea People institutional innovations. The Netherlands — a republic of merchants that invented the modern stock market, the central bank, and multinational corporations before anyone else. The United States — the Sea People tradition taken to its logical extreme: individual rights, the market as primary social organizer, the Constitution as contract between citizens. Singapore — pure harbor logic, existing entirely through being the most reliable, open, rule-governed trading node in Southeast Asia.

Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan contain strong Sea People dimensions in their trading and manufacturing cultures — though all three also contain significant River People institutional structures.

## The River People nations

China — the most complete River People civilization in the world. The examination system, the Confucian hierarchy, the bureaucratic state, the concept of tianxia, the emphasis on collective harmony — all River People characteristics developed to their fullest extent over two thousand years of continuous civilizational logic. Russia — River People shaped by the Eurasian steppe geography. The vast agricultural plains, the need for centralized control, the deep suspicion of Sea People cosmopolitanism as an existential threat to social cohesion. Iran — River People with a specifically Persian civilizational flavor, the first civilization to develop sophisticated bureaucratic administration at continental scale. Germany and France — both primarily River People in dominant orientation, though with significant Sea People dimensions in their trading cities and constitutional traditions.

## The Mountain People

There are no major nation-states primarily organized around Mountain People values — because the nation-state is itself a River People or Sea People institutional form. The Mountain People's communities are minorities within states designed by and for the other two poles.

Bhutan comes closest — deliberately small, explicitly organized around the preservation of its specific cultural and spiritual tradition. The Gross National Happiness index is a Mountain People response to the River People's GDP and the Sea People's market metrics.

The most important Mountain People political formations are not nation-states but communities within larger states — the Sami in Scandinavia, the indigenous nations of the Americas, the Adivasi of India, the Aboriginal communities of Australia, the Ainu of Japan, the diverse communities of the Pacific Islands maintaining oral traditions and sacred landscape relationships within political frameworks designed for other purposes.

## The global diagnostic

At planetary scale the Sea People coalition is dominant — the global trading system, the dollar reserve currency, the internet, international contract law are all Sea People institutional expressions operating globally.

The River People coalition is resurgent — China, Russia, and Iran explicitly rejecting Sea People universalism and asserting the right of hierarchical civilizational states to organize their own affairs. This is a genuine reaction to real Sea People overreach — the commodification of everything, the elimination of national sovereignty, the cultural dissolution that comes with deep commercial integration.

The Mountain People are in crisis — climate change is the Mountain People's existential emergency, the direct result of both the Sea People's commercial extraction logic and the River People's industrial development logic treating the sacred landscape as a resource. The Mountain People have no institutional voice at the global level commensurate with the scale of the threat.

## What each needs

The Sea People coalition needs River People long-term thinking and Mountain People ecological limits as constraints on commercial short-termism and environmental destruction.

The River People coalition needs Sea People individual rights and rule of law as constraints on hierarchical corruption and the suppression of dissent.

The Mountain People need both coalitions to stop treating their sacred landscapes as resources and their cultural traditions as obstacles to development.

The world needs what the Kojiki was attempting to build — a framework in which all three traditions are recognized as legitimate, none claims to be the universal framework containing the others, and the synthesis is maintained not because any single pole won but because all three are still rotating.

Three forces. One center. The system alive in the tension between them.

The mitsudomoe at planetary scale.
