# The World Today

The three-pole framework is not just a historical tool. It is a diagnostic — a way of identifying which pole is dominant in any given civilisation, which poles are being suppressed, and what the consequences of that imbalance are likely to be.

Every nation contains all three poles. What follows describes dominant orientations — which pole's values, institutions, and trust systems have most thoroughly shaped each national culture. The mapping is approximate and the categories are ideal types rather than precise descriptions of complex realities.

**The Sea People nations:**

The United Kingdom — historically the most complete Sea People civilisation. Common law, parliamentary democracy, the financial system, the stock market — all Sea People institutional innovations. The Netherlands — a republic of merchants that invented the modern stock market, the central bank, and multinational corporations before anyone else. The United States — the Sea People tradition taken to its logical extreme: individual rights, the market as primary social organiser, the Constitution as contract between citizens. Singapore — pure harbour logic, existing entirely through being the most reliable, open, rule-governed trading node in Southeast Asia.

Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan contain strong Sea People dimensions in their trading and manufacturing cultures — though all three also contain significant River People institutional structures.

**The River People nations:**

China — the most complete River People civilisation in the world. The examination system, the Confucian hierarchy, the bureaucratic state, the concept of tianxia, the emphasis on collective harmony — all River People characteristics developed to their fullest extent over two thousand years of continuous civilisational logic. Russia — River People shaped by the Eurasian steppe geography. The vast agricultural plains, the need for centralised control, the deep suspicion of Sea People cosmopolitanism as an existential threat to social cohesion. Iran — River People with a specifically Persian civilisational flavour, the first civilisation to develop sophisticated bureaucratic administration at continental scale. Germany and France — both primarily River People in dominant orientation, though with significant Sea People dimensions in their trading cities and constitutional traditions.

**The Mountain People:**

There are no major nation-states primarily organised around Mountain People values — because the nation-state is itself a River People or Sea People institutional form. The Mountain People's communities are minorities within states designed by and for the other two poles.

Bhutan comes closest — deliberately small, explicitly organised around the preservation of its specific cultural and spiritual tradition. The Gross National Happiness index is a Mountain People response to the River People's GDP and the Sea People's market metrics.

The most important Mountain People political formations are not nation-states but communities within larger states — the Sami in Scandinavia, the indigenous nations of the Americas, the Adivasi of India, the Aboriginal communities of Australia, the Ainu of Japan, the diverse communities of the Pacific Islands maintaining oral traditions and sacred landscape relationships within political frameworks designed for other purposes.

**Two confirming cases:**

Two civilisations outside the framework's original test cases are worth naming directly, because both were used to stress-test the theory and both held up. Indian caste society sorts cleanly as River: trust and obligation attach to hereditary category rather than the verified person, dharma is explicitly indexed to one's station, and the characteristic failure — hierarchy justified as cosmic order, extracting without being answerable to the people underneath it — is as complete an example of River's inversion as exists anywhere.

Islamic civilisation looked at first like a counterexample. The ummah is a universalist trust community — Sea-coded — that emerged from deep desert nomadic terrain, the paradigm Mountain geography. On a geography-first reading that shouldn't happen. It resolves once geography is treated as a driver of group size and stranger-contact rather than a direct cause: Arabia had real, tribal Mountain trust exactly as the terrain would predict, and separately a caravan-trade stranger-contact problem that no state reached far enough to solve with contract law. The hawala money-transfer system and the ummah's shared oath answered that problem with a Sea-style mechanism running on religious sanction instead of legal enforcement — impersonal, indifferent to who the person is, binding on anyone who recognises the same authority. Read this way, Islamic civilisation is not an exception to the framework. It is one of its clearest confirmations.

**The global diagnostic:**

At planetary scale the Sea People coalition is dominant — the global trading system, the dollar reserve currency, the internet, international contract law are all Sea People institutional expressions operating globally.

The River People coalition is resurgent — China, Russia, and Iran explicitly rejecting Sea People universalism and asserting the right of hierarchical civilisational states to organise their own affairs. This is a genuine reaction to real Sea People overreach — the commodification of everything, the elimination of national sovereignty, the cultural dissolution that comes with deep commercial integration.

The Mountain People are in crisis — climate change is the Mountain People's existential emergency, the direct result of both the Sea People's commercial extraction logic and the River People's industrial development logic treating the sacred landscape as a resource. The Mountain People have no institutional voice at the global level commensurate with the scale of the threat.

**What each needs:**

The Sea People coalition needs River People long-term thinking and Mountain People ecological limits as constraints on commercial short-termism and environmental destruction.

The River People coalition needs Sea People individual rights and rule of law as constraints on hierarchical corruption and the suppression of dissent.

The Mountain People need both coalitions to stop treating their sacred landscapes as resources and their cultural traditions as obstacles to development.

The world needs what the Kojiki was attempting to build — a framework in which all three traditions are recognised as legitimate, none claims to be the universal framework containing the others, and the synthesis is maintained not because any single pole won but because all three are still rotating.

Three forces. One centre. The system alive in the tension between them.

The mitsudomoe at planetary scale.
