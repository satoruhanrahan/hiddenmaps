If you are being criticised, punished, or hated, usually it would be because you are doing something to the detriment of someone else, but sometimes this is not the case. There is a type of social aggression that happens not because anyone is worse off in any way, but because life has become easier, and you have noticed it earlier than the others.

You may not be following social norms, or being "carefree". Refusing things that others have accepted as unavoidable duties. You are not hurting anyone, and yet they feel uncomfortable. Maybe even mad. Why is this? They are not bad or stupid people. They are following a natural and logical process that has worked for humanity.

## The Sunken Cost

When a child or adult accepts a painful rule — for example grinding overwork, submission to authority, or following the social norms of the group — it is not as simple as accepting it and moving on. A justification system needs to be built around it. The effort and sacrifice has to mean something. So the mind creates a belief around it: this is what responsible people do, this is what this situation requires of people, it is simply how life works.

The justifying belief is not dishonest. It has a real and important function when life truly does require people to act in a certain way. It makes it bearable, maybe even meaningful. The person gets to feel that the cost they are paying is necessary and the right thing to do. But what happens when conditions change and the effort and sacrifice is no longer needed?

## When Conditions Change

People do not constantly analyse their beliefs and coping mechanisms. It is cheap, easy, and safe for the human brain to form these justifications and just keep following them. So even after conditions improve, human behaviour does not change, sometimes for generations. During this period, many thinking people will realise the gap between reality and these justification beliefs, and simply stop following them.

This creates an issue. These free people are not following the "necessary" duties and yet nothing bad is happening to them. They seem happy, and are not causing any obvious harm to anyone. What is happening here? It can mean one of two things. Either the entire justification framework — the story that gives the suffering meaning — is wrong, or this free person is doing something wrong. The mind does not want to accept the first conclusion, so it tends to make the second conclusion.

This is why people can be completely convinced that you are selfish, irresponsible, naive, or secretly causing harm that others have not noticed yet, even though you are not. The apparent absence of any harmdoing just irritates them even more. Their mind may have to come up with wild accusations and theories, just so their internal justification beliefs can still make sense. There is a great resistance to confronting the possibility that years of your own suffering was meaningless.

## Jesus and the Pharisees

History is full of good figures who were wrongly oppressed. Many of these individuals were simply more adapted to the times than the collective was. Whether you believe he is God or not, Jesus was living the way humans should be living instead of how they were actually living. He didn't just preach kindness, he acted from internal authority, ignored scripts that others had sacrificed to follow, and reminded people of the spirit and meaning behind the rules.

The Pharisees who condemned Jesus built their identity on the rigorous following of rules. Witnessing Jesus breaking their rules triggered their coping mechanisms in an immense and dark way, despite Jesus doing nothing but good for people. As humanity has progressed through history, we needed fewer rules and gained more freedoms. The Bible shows this clearly. But humanity can at times take three steps forward and one or two steps back. Many modern churchgoers today resemble the Pharisees more than Jesus.

## How To Respond

Understanding this mechanism allows you to not take this anger personally. It is not you who is making them mad. It is the gap between reality and their model of the world that is making them mad. By simply living your life you are inadvertently reminding them of the reality that they cannot accept. Hopefully they may eventually realise that nothing you did actually bothered them. It was the silent and burning question within themselves: was the cost you paid actually necessary?
