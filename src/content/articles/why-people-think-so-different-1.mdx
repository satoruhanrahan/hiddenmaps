*For anyone with roots or experiences in two different cultures, one thing tends to grapple their minds. Why do these people think and act so differently?*

---

There are many reasons. But some reasons are less known than others, and some make a bigger impact than people realise. Two factors I believe that make a big impact are geography and survival. I have personal roots and experience living in both Japan and Australia and so I can feel the differences between the cultures of these two countries very deeply.

## What the Difference Feels Like

For anyone familiar enough with these two cultures, the contrast between them is noticeable and stark. In Japan, people have a collectivist mindset. One in which you are expected to "read the air". This is to understand what everyone around you is doing and feeling, so that you can match them in order to keep everything flowing as expected, without anyone feeling disturbed or annoyed.

In Australia the vibe is very different. Arriving into Australia from Japan, I feel a sense of freedom and release from the grip of having to read and follow this "air". In Australia, everything feels a little bit more chaotic. People are much more independent, and each person is expected to naturally think and behave differently, sometimes even in opposition of each other's values and beliefs. Both systems result in good and bad things.

## Two Main Reasons for the Difference

If I had to give two main reasons that could explain why this difference exists, they would be the floodplains of China, and the US Commodore Matthew Perry's black ships. Not the guy from the TV series Friends, but the 19th century naval officer. Let's start with the floodplains. Floodplains allow the farming of rice, which started in China and eventually found its way to Japan. China's culture and politics have made a huge impact on Japan over its history, and rice farming is one of the important factors.

## The Floodplains

Rice farming requires group effort and coordination, maintaining paddies, and making sure everyone does what is expected of them. This contrasts with wheat farming, sea fishing, and sea trading, which have had a bigger influence on Western culture. Wheat farming does not require as much group effort or coordination. Traders are expected to find new ideas, methods, and ways of thinking to get ahead of the competition. This fosters a culture of individualism and innovative thinking.

## The Black Ships

The black ships that arrived in Japan in the 1850s threatened and forced Japan to sign unfair treaties that allowed the Western nations to trade with Japan. Every Japanese person is familiar with this event. It is what shocked the nation into emergency mode. To avoid the fate of neighbouring China, which was carved up into colonies, Japan had to form a strict, hierarchical, and obedient society in which everyone worked together to modernise, strengthen, and defend Japan from the foreign "barbarians".

These two are some of the important factors out of many that have shaped the differences between Japan and Australia. In the next article I will go more in depth into what I call the floodplain cultures and the fractured sea cultures.
